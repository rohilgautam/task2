About Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, was an Indian aerospace scientist who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. His journey from humble beginnings to becoming one of the most respected leaders in India is nothing short of inspirational.

As the chief scientist at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India". His work on the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998 was instrumental in establishing India as a nuclear power.

In 2002, Dr. Kalam became the President of India, where he was affectionately referred to as the "People's President". Throughout his life, he remained a staunch advocate for education, youth empowerment, and nation-building. His autobiography, Wings of Fire, continues to inspire millions of young minds to dream big and work hard to achieve their goals.

Achievements & Awards

  • Padma Bhushan (1981) and Padma Vibhushan (1990)
  • Bharat Ratna (1997) - India's highest civilian honor
  • Played a key role in the development of India's ballistic missile and space rocket technology
  • Authored numerous books including Wings of Fire and Ignited Minds
  • Inspiration to millions of students and young scientists
